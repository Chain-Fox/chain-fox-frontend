Chain Fox Wallet Security

At Chain Fox, we prioritize the security of your digital assets. This page explains how we interact with your wallet and the security measures we've implemented to protect your assets.

How We Use Your Wallet

Chain Fox only requests wallet connections for the following purposes:

  • To verify your ownership of the wallet through message signing
  • To display your wallet balance
  • To provide personalized services based on your wallet address

We never request transaction approvals or transfers without your explicit consent.

Our Security Practices

When you connect your wallet to Chain Fox:

  • We only request a signature for authentication purposes, never for transactions
  • All signature requests include clear explanations of their purpose
  • We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data
  • We never store your private keys or seed phrases
  • All wallet interactions happen client-side in your browser

Supported Wallets

Chain Fox supports various Solana wallets, including:

  • Phantom
  • Solflare
  • OKX Wallet
  • Backpack
  • Other Solana-compatible wallets

False Positive Security Alerts

Some wallet extensions may occasionally flag Chain Fox as suspicious due to our wallet connection requests. This is a false positive. Our code is open source and can be audited on our GitHub repository.

If you receive a security alert, you can safely proceed with the connection. We only request the minimum permissions necessary for our application to function.

Reporting Security Issues

If you encounter any security issues or have concerns about our wallet integration, please contact us at security@chain-fox.com.
